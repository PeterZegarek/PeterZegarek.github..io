Recently, I had the opportunity to present my work (on the research project) at a regional conference, CCSCNE, and my partner and I won second place!

What got me into Computer Science?

It's actually a bit hard to say what the initial thing was that got me into CS. I was always loosely interested in the field but nothing had entirely sold me on it. In high school I took AP Computer Science Principles, which was a cool class, but we didn't do much coding in there at all, which meant that I still didn't really know if CS was going to be a fit for me. I toured Quinnipiac, and the CS faculty completely sold my family and I on the school. I committed here as a CS major, and then once I started taking classes, things really flew away!

The more and more I learned about the field and the more projects I took on, the more interested I got. Whether it was trying to speed up an algorithm from O(n^2) time to O(n) time, or re-learning how to use Java Swing every few months because I would forget, it was all so much fun! I find myself wanting to continue learning more and more about CS as the days go by. Attending hackathons has also been an awesome experience. I get to try random things and spend a whole day working on them, with a chance of prizes at the end! In my most recent hackathon (April 2024), my group won second place!

So while there isn't really a specific catalyst that got me interested in CS, it was just the ins and outs of daily things that really made me fall in love with the field. That all started during my first semester of college.
